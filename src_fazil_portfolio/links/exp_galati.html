Responsibilities & Technical Experience

1

Production Line Operation — Automated Equipment

Operate and monitor high-speed automated production lines including: spreading line, shredding machines, bagging machines, automated weighing systems, and conveyor systems. Lines run at 60–120 packages/minute requiring continuous vigilance for process deviations. Monitor fill weights, bag seal integrity, and product consistency in real time.

2

Sensor & Actuator Fault Diagnosis

First-level fault diagnosis on industrial sensors and actuators: proximity sensors (inductive, detecting metal jam), photoelectric sensors (detecting product presence/absence on conveyor), solenoid valves (pneumatic actuator control), belt drives, and motor overloads. Diagnose using indicator lights, fault codes, and physical inspection. Resolve common faults independently; escalate complex faults to maintenance engineers with accurate symptom documentation.

3

Preventive Maintenance (PM) Execution

Execute scheduled weekly and monthly PM routines as trained by the engineering team: lubrication, belt tension checks, sensor alignment verification, seal integrity inspection, and cleaning. Maintain complete PM records and maintenance logs — traceability required for GMP food safety compliance. PM adherence prevents unplanned downtime and extends equipment lifespan.

4

Process Parameter Monitoring & Adjustment

Monitor real-time process control parameters: fill weights (g, within ±2g tolerance), line speeds (packages/min), temperature setpoints (cold chain). Adjust control parameters to maintain product quality within specification. Document out-of-spec events in non-conformance log for QA review.

5

LOTO, OHSA & HACCP Compliance

Apply Lockout/Tagout (LOTO) procedures for all equipment maintenance activities — verifying zero energy state before physical contact. Comply with OHSA (Ontario) workplace safety requirements. Follow HACCP food safety protocols for allergen control, foreign material prevention, and sanitation. Collaborate with QA on non-conformance documentation and corrective actions.

  • ▸ Process control intuition — understanding real-world process parameter drift, tolerance windows, and setpoint adjustment is directly relevant to PLC programming and process control engineering roles at Siemens, Rockwell Automation, and Honeywell.

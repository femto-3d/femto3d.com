Dual-Stage Bio-Potential Signal Amplifier

Overview

Designed a 2-layer PCB in Autodesk Eagle for conditioning bio-potential signals (EMG and EEG) from surface electrodes before feeding them to the ARM Cortex ADC. Bio-potential signals are extremely weak (10 µV – 5 mV for EMG; 1–100 µV for EEG) and heavily contaminated by 50/60 Hz mains interference, common-mode noise from electrode-skin contact, and motion artifacts.

The circuit uses the TL074 quad op-amp IC to implement a two-stage amplification and filtering chain that boosts the useful signal by 60–80 dB while rejecting power line noise and DC offset — delivering a clean, ADC-compatible output (0–3.3V range).

This PCB was a sub-component of the BCI-Controlled Bionic Arm flagship project, but represents a standalone hardware design deliverable with direct applicability to medical device, sensor IC, and wearable electronics development.

Circuit Design — Stage by Stage

1

Stage 1 — Instrumentation Amplifier (Differential Input)

The first amplifier stage is configured as a differential instrumentation amplifier using two op-amp sections of the TL074. Differential topology rejects common-mode signals (power line noise, EMI) with a CMRR > 80 dB. The gain resistor sets Stage 1 gain to 100× (40 dB), amplifying the µV-range differential bio-potential while keeping common-mode noise unchanged. A high-input-impedance buffer protects the electrode-skin interface from loading.

2

Stage 2 — Active Bandpass Filter (Sallen-Key Topology)

The second stage uses the remaining two sections of the TL074 to implement an active bandpass filter. The filter is configured as a cascaded high-pass (20 Hz cutoff) and low-pass (500 Hz cutoff) Sallen-Key stage — targeting the EMG frequency band (20–500 Hz). For EEG acquisition mode, component values are swapped (selectable via jumper) for an 8–30 Hz alpha/beta band. Additional 50 Hz notch achieved through component ratio tuning. Total Stage 2 gain: 10× (20 dB).

3

DC Offset & Level Shifting

Bio-potential amplifiers operating from a single supply (3.3V for ARM compatibility) require mid-supply biasing. A precision voltage divider (1.65V virtual ground) biases the amplifier chain mid-rail, allowing bipolar signal excursions while keeping the output within ADC range. Output signal: 1.65V ± 1.2V under full EMG activation, cleanly within the STM32 ADC's 0–3.3V range.

4

PCB Layout — Autodesk Eagle (2-Layer)

Laid out the 2-layer PCB in Autodesk Eagle with careful attention to analog layout rules: (1) Ground plane on bottom layer for shielding; (2) Input trace kept short with guard rings to prevent capacitive coupling; (3) Decoupling capacitors placed within 2mm of each op-amp supply pin; (4) Electrode connector pads separated from digital circuit sections. Board dimensions: 45mm × 35mm. DRC passed with zero errors before fabrication.

5

Validation & Testing

Board validated on oscilloscope with a 1 mV pp, 50 Hz test signal injected at the input. Output measured at 1V pp at 50 Hz with >90% attenuation (expected for notch filter), confirming filter operation. Live EMG test with surface electrodes on forearm: clean signal visible on oscilloscope with muscle contraction — noise floor <50 µV after filtering. ADC input confirmed stable and noise-free during Bionic Arm integration testing.

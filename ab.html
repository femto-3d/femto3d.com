About Me

I'm a Machine Learning Engineer with 3+ years of industry experience building AI-driven systems and production data pipelines. I'm proficient in Python and the full ML stack — from feature engineering and model training to deployment, drift monitoring, and CI/CD lifecycle management.

My work spans the financial analytics space: I've built a fraud detection model on 284K real banking transactions (AUC-ROC 0.982), an LSTM autoencoder for customer spending anomaly detection (100% recall, zero false negatives), and an NLP auto-tagging pipeline with text-to-speech — a capability explicitly listed in the BMO job description. Every model I've shipped includes explainability (SHAP), experiment tracking (MLflow), and automated drift monitoring.

At IDP Education, I applied ML to a 500K+ record dataset — building supervised models for applicant risk scoring, ETL pipelines for a centralized data lake, and A/B testing frameworks that fed directly into business decision-making. I understand that a model that can't be explained or reproduced under audit is not a finished model — especially in a regulated financial environment.

I'm currently completing my MEng in Electrical & Computer Engineering at the University of Windsor, available for hybrid work in Toronto, and excited about BMO's commitment to responsible AI — interpretability, transparency, and bias mitigation are already baked into how I build.
